WORKPLACE EXPOSURE LIMITS — SITE REFERENCE EXTRACT
(Internal training summary; concept-level, modelled on HSE EH40/2005.)

A Workplace Exposure Limit (WEL) is the maximum concentration of an airborne
substance, averaged over a specified reference period, to which workers may be
exposed. GB WELs are listed in HSE document EH40/2005 and are enforced under the
Control of Substances Hazardous to Health Regulations 2002 (COSHH).

Two reference periods are used:

  * Long-term WEL  — an 8-hour time-weighted average (TWA). This is the headline
    limit for chronic exposure. The 8-hour TWA weights each measured concentration
    by the time spent at it, averaged across a nominal 8-hour (480-minute) working
    day. Periods of no exposure within the shift count as zero and still occupy
    their share of the 480 minutes.

  * Short-term WEL — a 15-minute TWA (the STEL), used to control brief excursions.
    It is a SEPARATE limit and is not interchangeable with the 8-hour TWA.

A naive arithmetic mean of the sampled concentrations is NOT the 8-hour TWA: the
durations differ, so the readings must be time-weighted.

Selected long-term WELs (8-hour TWA), fictional substances for training:

  Substance   Long-term WEL (8-hr TWA)   Short-term WEL (15-min STEL)
