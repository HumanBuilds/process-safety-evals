CONTROL OF SUBSTANCES HAZARDOUS TO HEALTH — SITE OVERVIEW NOTE

COSHH (the Control of Substances Hazardous to Health Regulations 2002, as amended)
requires employers to assess the risks from hazardous substances and prevent or
adequately control exposure. The eight principles of good control practice apply;
they are not ranked. Health surveillance may be required under regulation 11 where
an identifiable disease or adverse health effect may be linked to exposure.

This overview is general policy context. It does NOT contain any exposure limits,
sampling data, or numerical values, and is not sufficient on its own to decide
whether a measured exposure complies with a workplace exposure limit.
